WORLD NEWS HEADLINES - MAY 24, 2026

1. Geopolitics & Conflict
   - Ukraine Conflict: Russia conducted one of its largest air attacks on Kyiv, using a combination of hypersonic missiles and drones. The assault resulted in at least four deaths and over 100 injuries, causing severe damage.
   - U.S.-Iran Negotiations: Intense diplomatic efforts are underway to negotiate a potential 60-day ceasefire and security agreement in the Strait of Hormuz. The proposal involves reopening the strait and lifting restrictions on Iranian oil sales, though President Donald Trump emphasized that final access depends on resolving remaining terms.
   - Pakistan-China Diplomacy: High-level diplomatic talks commenced in Beijing as the Prime Minister of Pakistan arrived in China for bilateral discussions.

2. Domestic Security & Incidents
   - White House Shooting: A significant security incident occurred near the White House in Washington, D.C. A gunman was shot and killed after exchanging gunfire with Secret Service agents. No other injuries were reported, but the area was briefly locked down.
   - Pakistan Railway Blast: A deadly blast targeted a railway station or train in Quetta, Pakistan, leading to multiple casualties.
   - Spain Clashes: Police at Bilbao airport arrested four individuals following clashes related to the return of activists from a Gaza-bound flotilla.

3. Public Health
   - Ebola Outbreak in Central Africa: The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the Ebola virus outbreak (Bundibugyo strain) in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) and Uganda a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. The outbreak is centered in Ituri province, DRC, with hundreds of cases and over 180 deaths reported.

4. Sports & Entertainment
   - Passing of NASCAR Champion Kyle Busch: The racing world is mourning the loss of two-time NASCAR Cup Series champion Kyle Busch, who passed away on May 21, 2026, at age 41 due to complications from severe pneumonia and sepsis.
